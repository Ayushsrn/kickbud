About

Kickbud is the upgrade to the ancient game of shuttlecock. Kickbud is both a noun and a verb. To Kickbud is to keep the Kickbud off the floor. The Kickbud is aerodynamically designed to improve flight time and landing, making it easy for beginners. It is made of 4 feathers placed adjacently to each other, attached to a rubber pad.

Kickbud improves foot-eye coordination, reflexes, first touches and mood.

How to Play

The aim of the game is to keep the Kickbud off the floor. The game can be played solo, with a partner, or with a team of up to 8 people.

Solo players can juggle the Kickbud and show off their freestyle skills. 2-4 players can group themselves and play competitively to see who lets it drop first. 2-8 players can stand in a circle to freestyle and keep the Kickbud in air for as long as possible.

  • Things to carry – Kickbud.
  • Where to play – Basement, Terrace, Living room, Soccer field, Park, Badminton Court… Basically ANYWHERE. Avoid extremely windy locations for a better experience.
  • Pro tip – Use the inside of your heel as a point of contact with the Kickbud. It helps the player have more control over the direction of the Kickbud.

PS - The game is young, and there are no set rules. Feel free to mix it up and experiment with different ways of playing Kickbud.
